Foods that are *formulated* (i.e. do not undergo feeding tests), are more often designed to meet the needs of a specific life stage. Chef Michael's is the only pet food (dog or cat), that I have ever found that has undergone feeding trials AND is designed for a specific life stage.<br /><br />The importance of feeding trials, formulations, and life stages is debatable, and in the end is an individual decision.<br /><br />Finally, despite what some people claim, it is not possible to assess the quality of a food from the ingredient list. Ingredient descriptions, for example any listing of a meat product or by-product, are vague and there can be huge variation in the quality of a product with the same name from one food to another. In the same vein, claims that certain ingredients shouldn't be in dog food should be taken with a grain (or large jar) of salt. When chosing a food for your pet, talk to your vet, don't believe a stranger on the internet with unknown qualifications.<br /><br />As for me, although my dog enjoyed Chef Michael's, both the calorie content and price are too high to make it her normal dog food.
