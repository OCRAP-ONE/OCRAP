review/text: I just made these today, and I have to say that I'm pleasantly surprised.  These will definitely go on my list of guilty pleasures.  The taste and consistency are such that you wouldn't be able to tell that these are gluten-free if you hadn't seen the box and made them yourself.<br /><br />Just a note for those who might try to veganize these.  I'm not vegan, but I have done some vegan baking, and I've also taken a food science class or two.  Trying to do your standard vegan substitutions for this mix probably won't work.  The non-glutinous flours and starches in this won't react the same as if there were regular all purpose flour in it.  That's one of the main reasons that this mix calls for the use of butter instead of oil, like a regular box brownie mix.  It's the saturated fat in the butter that's helping to hold these gluten-free flours and starches together.  Saturated fat is solid at room temperature.  Oil is not.  It's that saturated fat that's helping to give these brownies their structure.  Using Earth Balance margarine might help, because it does have saturated fat.  However, it doesn't have as much of it as butter.  You might need to get a bit creative by using half Earth Balance and half coconut butter.  Coconut butter has almost twice the saturated fat of butter, but Earth Balance is a bit more solid at higher temps.  The combination of the two might achieve the desired effect.  Also, using Ener-G egg won't provide enough emollients for this mix.  I would recommend using some flax goo (Google flax egg if not sure) in addition to the Ener-G egg.  The Ener-G egg will give the brownies some rise and the flax goo will add more moisture and additional cohesion to make sure that these things don't just crumble when you cut them.<br /><br />I haven't tried the above recommendations yet, so I can't give a 100% guarantee on the results, but it's a decent starting point to experiment.  The end result, done properly, is definitely worth the effort.
